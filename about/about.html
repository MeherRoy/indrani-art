About Indrani Roy Choudhury

Indrani Roy Choudhury is a passionate writer and poet whose creative expressions touch upon various aspects of life, culture, and spirituality. Born and educated in India, she completed her schooling from Loreto Convent Ranchi in 1974. She completed B.A (1980) and M.A (1983) from Jadavpur University.


Her works reflect a deep connection to Indian culture and traditions, while also exploring universal themes of love, nature, and human relationships. From pieces about festivals like Onam and Holi to thoughtful reflections on contemporary issues, her poetry spans a wide range of subjects.


As a working woman and a mother, she has always strived to spread love and joy through her writing. Her involvement with the GIASOWA Learning Center demonstrates her commitment to social causes, particularly in education and empowering underprivileged children.


Her poetry often draws inspiration from various sources - from the simple beauty of roses in her garden to profound spiritual experiences. She has been particularly influenced by the works of Rabindranath Tagore, as evident in her compositions like 'My Union with the Lord' and 'Prayer To Mother Goddess'.
